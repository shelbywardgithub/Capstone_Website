Password Strength

Password strength is a measure of the effectiveness of a password against guessing or brute-force attacks. Password strength is determined by three factors - length, complexity, and unpredictability. Using strong passwords lowers overall risk of a security breach and the effectiveness of a password of a given strength is strongly determined by the design and implementation of the factors (knowledge, ownership, inherence).

File Identification

It can be difficult to know whether a file is safe to download, as many websites claim to have the correct file, but you must open the file to be sure. By the time you do, it may be too late to protect your computer from malware and viruses.

Unsecure Networks

An insecure wireless connection is one that can be accessed without a password. Public networks are available in many places, including cafes. Although these public wireless access points provide free Internet access, using them on public Internet is risky. If your home Internet is open, you should consider securing wireless access in order to protect your data and avoid legal trouble.

Phishing Awareness

Phishing is where a target or targets are contacted by email, telephone or text message by someone posing as a legitimate institution to lure individuals into providing sensitive data such as personally identifiable information, banking and credit card details, and passwords.
