NETWORK PRIVACY

A Risk of Networks.

READ TIME: 3 MINUTES

There are two types of networks secure and unsecured. If you are in a library, a small restaurant where coffee is served, or an airport and do not have an internet connection. If you try and connect to a public WIFI that is not a password protected chances are you are putting yourself in danger.

Say you are doing any sort of sensitive activity on the unsecured network including shopping, banking, or emails. The information could be exposed to anyone on the network who is looking for sensitive information.

One of the most common ways a hacker will infiltrate your information on a public network is Man in Middle Attacks(MITM). MITM Attacks intercept communications and data being shared between the server and the client. The hacker then can display their versions of a site and or messages. Another method and variation of a MITM attack is a fake WIFI hotspot. If you join a fake access point all data transmitted over the network goes directly to the hacker. These are two simple ways for a hacker to trick you into handing over your sensitive information.

You do not have to avoid using public WIFI altogether. Whenever possible connect to familiar networks and verify the password with staff. Also, ensure that any website you log on to has HTTPS instead of HTTP. HTTP is not encrypted or safe connections. For extra security use a VPN or virtual private network to encrypt your data traveling to or from the devices. The VPN will connect you to a private server for a low cost and protect your information.
